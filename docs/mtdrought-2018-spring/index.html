2018 Spring Outlook

Welcome to the April 2018 Montana Drought & Climate newsletter. We are working to provide and interpret climate data for agricultural producers in Montana. With our seasonal newsletters, we hope to transform existing forecasts and projections into useful climate information that meet producer needs. Please send us your feedback!

Here’s what you’ll find in this newsletter:

Conditions — Understanding current conditions aids in predicting future conditions. This section provides an overview of what happened over the season and allows producers to reflect on years past in comparison.

Forecast — The seasonal forecast is generally the most frequently requested dataset by producers. Here Temperature, Precipitation, Drought and influential global air circulation patterns are discussed in regional detail.

2040–2069 Outlook — Modeling allows for a better idea of what mid-century conditions could look like in Montana. Comparing these projected conditions to current or past conditions gives an improved understanding of how to respond to a changing climate.

In Brief

  • Montana experienced a cold and wet February and winter, resulting in near-record high snowpack across the western mountains and eastern plains.
  • Significant precipitation alleviated drought conditions that remained from summer 2017. As a result, drought has contracted to northeastern Montana, with severe drought remaining in Daniels, Roosevelt, and Sheridan counties.
  • The La Niña conditions that brought above average winter precipitation and cooler temperatures are expected to dissipate to ENSO neutral conditions by early spring.
  • The spring outlook for temperature is cooler than normal across eastern, central, and northwestern portions of the state. Equal chances of above, below, or normal spring temperatures are expected for the southwestern portions of the state.
  • The spring outlook for precipitation is for wetter than normal conditions in portions of northeastern and northcentral Montana. Equal chances of above, below, or normal precipitation is forecast for the rest of the state.

About Montana Drought & Climate
and the Montana Climate Office

Montana Drought and Climate is a USDA-funded project of the Montana Climate Office (MCO) at the W.A. Franke College of Forestry & Conservation at the University of Montana, in collaboration with the Montana State University Extension Service. The MCO is an independent state-designated body that provides Montanans with high-quality, timely, relevant, and scientifically-based climate information and services. We strive to be a credible and expert source of information for decision makers that rely on the most current information on climate to make important decisions. It is also the role of the MCO to assist stakeholders in interpreting climate information or adapting climate products to their needs.

Production of this newsletter is supported by Water for Agriculture Challenge Area grant no. 2017-67027-26313 from the USDA National Institute of Food and Agriculture.
